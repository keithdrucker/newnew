You are enhancing an existing ITSM platform to support multi‑year planning across Initiatives, Projects, and the Risk Register.
Your task is to implement a Planning Year filter that supports planning across time without hiding open work when the calendar year changes.

This feature must balance planning context with operational reality.

1️⃣ Scope & Constraints
Applies to:

Initiatives
Projects
Risk Register

Do NOT:

Add arbitrary date‑range pickers
Add fiscal‑year logic
Treat this as reporting or analytics
Hide open items due to year boundaries


2️⃣ Planning Year Filter – UI Placement

Place the filter in the top‑right of each module’s main view
Implement as a single dropdown

Label (Required)

Planning Year

Avoid:

Date Range
Reporting Period
Fiscal Year


3️⃣ Planning Year Range (Explicit)
The dropdown MUST be dynamically generated and strictly limited to:
📅 This Year
• 2026 (Current)

📁 Previous Years
• 2025
• 2024
• 2023

🚀 Future Years
• 2027
• 2028
• 2029

Rules:

Current calendar year selected by default
Show only the previous 3 calendar years
Show only the next 3 calendar years
No additional years outside this window


4️⃣ Authoritative Date Fields (Per Module)
Use exactly one authoritative date field per module:

Initiatives → Planned Start Year
Projects → Planned Start Year
Risk Register → Review / Decision Year

These fields define the Planning Year category, not visibility.

5️⃣ CRITICAL RULE: Open‑Item Carryover ✅
Golden Rule

Open items must always be visible in the current year, regardless of their Planning Year.

This rule is mandatory.

Visibility Logic (Authoritative)
When a user selects:
✅ Current Year (e.g., 2026)
Show:

✅ All items with Status ≠ Closed, regardless of Planning Year
✅ PLUS any items planned for the current year

This ensures:

Long‑running projects remain visible
Initiatives started last year but still open do not “disappear”
Active risks remain visible until explicitly closed


✅ Previous Year (e.g., 2025)
Show:

Items whose Planning Year = 2025
Includes both open and closed items
Does NOT auto‑include open items from other years

Purpose: historical review and audit context.

✅ Future Year (e.g., 2027)
Show:

Items whose Planning Year = 2027
Includes planned initiatives, projects, or risks
DO NOT include current open work unless it is planned for that year

Purpose: planning and roadmap visibility.

6️⃣ Creating Items for Future Years ✅
Users must be able to:

Create Initiatives for future years (within +3 years)
Create Projects planned for future years
Create Risks with future review/decision years

Rules:

New items default to the currently selected Planning Year
Users may override the year during creation (within allowed range)
Do NOT block future‑year creation


7️⃣ Defaults & Persistence

Default Planning Year = current calendar year
Preserve the selected Planning Year:

Across navigation within the session


On January 1:

Default filter rolls forward automatically
Year window shifts forward
No items are auto‑archived or hidden




8️⃣ UX Messaging (Required)
To avoid confusion, include contextual messaging.
Header / Helper Text Example

“Showing all open items, plus items planned for 2026.”

Empty State Example

“No initiatives planned for 2028 yet.”


9️⃣ Explicit Non‑Goals
This feature must NOT:

Affect SLA calculations
Affect performance reporting
Affect audit logs or history
Introduce budgeting, cost tracking, or finance logic
Introduce fiscal calendars

This is planning visibility, not analytics.

✅ Expected Outcome
After implementation:

Open Initiatives, Projects, and Risks are never hidden due to year rollover
Planning Year provides meaningful organization without risk
Future‑year planning feels intentional and supported
The system reflects real operational behavior

Do not introduce additional filters or year logic beyond what is defined here.

✅ Design Principle to Enforce

Status determines visibility. Planning Year determines grouping.
